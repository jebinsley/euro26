🍺
Evening: Traditional Beer Halls near Marienplatz
Top choices — no reservation needed:
· Hofbräuhaus (Am Platzl 9) — the most iconic beer hall in the world, 5 min from Marienplatz. Oompah band, 1-litre Maß, tourists and locals alike. A must.
· Augustiner Stammhaus (Neuhauser Str. 27) — Locals' favourite; fresh gravity-fed beer straight from wooden barrels. Less touristy than Hofbräuhaus. Excellent food.
· Löwenbräukeller (Stiglmaierplatz) — Grand 19th-century beer hall and garden, 10 min on U1 from Karlsplatz. Authentic, spacious, no reservations needed weekday evenings.
· Spatenhaus an der Oper (Residenzstr. 12) — Upscale, across from the Opera. Great Bavarian cuisine; a quieter setting for a special first dinner in Munich.
Recommendation Start at Augustiner Stammhaus for beer, then walk to Hofbräuhaus for the atmosphere

Tips & Know Before You Go

🍺 Löwenbräu-Festzelt Logistics
Arrive on time — tables released after 15 min. Lunch ends at 16:30 sharp; you must leave the tent. Vouchers: pick up at Hirschau (Mon–Thu, Aug 17–Sep 10) or at the tent office on the day (left of main entrance). EC card only — no credit cards.
🚄 Roma Termini vs. Tiburtina
The Nightjet departs Roma Tiburtina, not Termini. Take Metro Line B from Termini (~5 stops, 10 min, €1.50). Or take a taxi (~€10–15, 15 min). Plan to be at Tiburtina by 17:30 at the latest — trains in Italy run on time.
⛴️ Positano Departure Day
Sep 21 is your most logistically complex day: ferry + train + night train. Book the early morning ferry (7–8 AM) to give yourself a 3-hour buffer in Rome. Pack bags the night before — Positano streets are steep and narrow.
🏰 Sep 22 — Marienplatz Beer Halls
Arrive day — skip the festival crowds. Start at Augustiner Stammhaus (Neuhauser Str. 27) for Munich's best fresh-tap beer, then walk 10 min to the Hofbräuhaus for the atmosphere. Both walk-in, no reservation needed on Tuesday evenings.
